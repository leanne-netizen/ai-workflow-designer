Here's the actual story.

I run a wedding celebrant and planning business in Vietnam. I also hold a sales role inside a digital consultancy — someone else's business, not mine, which means I'm accountable to a second set of deadlines, clients, and moving parts without controlling the systems around me.

Both very different. Both full of the kind of repetitive, brain-draining admin that steals the first two hours of your day before you've done a single thing that matters.

So I built AI workflows and systems.

AI connected to a database, feeding me what I need, when I need it, in one place. Overnight activity pulled and sorted. Priorities surfaced. Follow-ups drafted. Tasks handled. And then a little message that says: all done, enjoy your day.

I've been running this inside my own businesses for months. It works. Not in a "the demo looked great" way. In a "I actually trust it with my morning" way.

Who this is for.

Solo business owners who are competent, busy, and tired of being the person who holds everything in their head.

The whole point of a good system is that it handles the repeatable stuff so the things that actually matter — your voice, your relationships, your judgment — stay yours.

That's what I build. Fast where speed is fine. Careful where it counts.
